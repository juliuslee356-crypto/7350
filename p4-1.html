Literature Review

What current research tells us about AI-Generated Content in journalism

01 — Introduction

What Is AIGC in Journalism?

AI-Generated Content (AIGC) refers to text, images, and multimedia created by artificial intelligence systems. In journalism, this includes automated news articles, data summaries, and AI-assisted reporting. Research interest has grown sharply, with a 172% increase in peer-reviewed publications recorded in 2024 alone, driven by the mainstream adoption of generative AI tools such as ChatGPT (Lim et al., 2025).

02 — Key Research Focus

What Studies Focus On

A systematic review of 72 peer-reviewed articles identified three major themes: efficiency gains from automation, credibility and trust challenges, and the changing roles of journalists. Ethics, misinformation, and public trust are identified as the field's most pressing unresolved problems (Lim et al., 2025).

03 — Major Research Findings

Key Findings

  • AIGC improves newsroom efficiency Controlled experiments show AI-authored articles are rated comparable to human content in clarity and quality, allowing newsrooms to scale output (Zheng & Liu, 2025).
  • AIGC spreads misinformation faster AI fake news websites increased over 1,000% between May 2023 and May 2024. Synthetic news spreads rapidly due to optimized fluency (Li & Yang, 2025).
  • AI news contains racial and gender bias All seven major language models tested produced news with measurable bias compared to original Reuters and New York Times articles (Lim et al., 2024).
  • Journalists shift to editorial roles Interviews with 21 journalists found AI handles routine tasks, pushing reporters toward verification and editorial judgment (Lim et al., 2025).
04 — Example Studies

Selected Peer-Reviewed Research

Lim et al. (2025)

Systematic review of 72 articles (2010–2025). Identifies ethics, misinformation, and trust as the field's core unresolved challenges.

Li & Yang (2025)

Randomized experiment (n=800) on AIGC labels. Found labels build user awareness but do not significantly change perceived credibility.

Jiang & Zhang (2024)

Analyzes ethical risks of AI cross-modal content generation, calling for transparency standards and regulatory frameworks.

Zheng & Liu (2025)

Compares AI-generated and human-written news under identical headlines, exploring patterns in AI journalistic output.

05 — Media Coverage Review

How Media Discusses AIGC

✦ Positive Views

  • Greater speed and news output
  • Cost savings for smaller outlets
  • Automation of data-heavy tasks
  • New storytelling possibilities

✦ Critical Views

  • Fast spread of misinformation
  • Racial and gender bias in AI output
  • Reduced editorial accountability
  • Risk of journalist job losses

Sentiment analysis of published research shows a mostly positive tone, but a notable spike in negative sentiment occurred in 2021 as misinformation and bias concerns grew (Lim et al., 2025).

06 — Summary

Conclusion

"AIGC presents both significant opportunities and serious challenges for journalism — demanding robust governance, editorial oversight, and continued interdisciplinary research."
