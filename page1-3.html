AIGC Key Terms Glossary

Essential vocabulary for understanding the intersection of advanced computation and modern media.

Technical Essentials for Journalists

Interaction

Prompt Engineering

Design and optimize the input text to guide AI to produce the best content that meets the news specifications.

Risk

AI Hallucination

AI confidently produces false or fabricated information. This is the biggest risk in the press, which requires strict manual verification.

Advanced Tech

RAG (Retrieval Enhanced Generation)

A technology that allows AI to retrieve facts from verified databases (such as newspaper archives) before answering, which can greatly reduce nonsense.

Ethics

Algorithmic Bias

Systematic errors that may exist in AI systems, such as discrimination against specific groups, require journalists to maintain a sense of accountability when using them.

Zero-Shot vs. Few-Shot Learning

These terms describe how we "teach" AI in the newsroom:

  • Zero sample: let AI perform the task directly (for example: please help me write a title).
  • Less sample: Provide AI with 3-5 excellent title cases before your newspaper, so that it can learn a specific editing style before creating.
