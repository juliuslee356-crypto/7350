Global Media AI Cases

Real applications from AP, BBC, Xinhua, Caixin and more

1. Associated Press – Automated Earnings Reports

Source: Associated Press Official AI Newsroom

The Automated Insights Wordsmith platform has been used to generate thousands of quarterly earnings reports of publicly traded companies since 2014 by the pioneer of automated journalism, the Associated Press (AP). This action increased their coverage significantly to more than 3,000 reports in a quarter as compared to an average of 300 reports annually. The AI platform will handle raw financial information, structure it into formatted articles, and continue the journalistic approach of AP, which will enable journalists to concentrate on deep analysis and investigative journalism.

The effectiveness of this project underscores the importance of AI in processing large data-driven reports effectively. Through the automation of repetitive jobs, AP offers coverage to the stock market in totality delivering timely information to the investors and the people. This human-in-the-middle approach, in which AI produces drafts and editors fact-check and polish them has become a norm in the field, showing a mutually beneficial partnership between technology and the human touch.

Source: https://www.ap.org/discover/artificial-intelligence

2. BBC Sport – AI Match Summaries & Highlights

Source: BBC Sport Official Website

BBC Sport has also implemented AI technology to automate match reports and highlight summaries, especially in the case of lower tiers of football leagues and sports events that were once given limited coverage. Real-time match data, such as goals, substitutions and key statistics, are fed into the AI system to produce concise and accurate reports a few minutes after a game has ended. This enables the BBC to report on a far wider scope of sporting events, and reach niche audiences as well as report on the full gamut of sports news.

BBC journalists review and edit the AI-generated content, and the final product will be of high quality. This not only boosts the amount of content, but improves the quality of coverage of less mainstream sports, making the sports media environment more inclusive. The application of AI by BBC can serve as an example of how technology can help to democratize sports journalism and make sure that all sports fans can receive timely and interesting content.

Source: https://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre

3. Xinhua News Agency – AI Anchor & “Kuai Bi Xiao Xin”

The largest state news media in China is the Xinhua News Agency, which has been leading in the use of AI in journalism. In 2015, it introduced "Kuai Bi Xiao Xin," an AI writing system that works with data-heavy news, like financial reports and sports updates. It can produce articles in Chinese and English in a matter of a few seconds, and this has greatly increased the productivity in the routine news coverage. This will enable Xinhua reporters to dedicate more time to detailed reporting and analysis.

In addition to text generation, Xinhua has also launched AI news anchors who are capable of delivering 24/7 presentations, like human presenters. The digital anchors are utilized to provide news updates on their social media and web pages and to keep on giving the updates on the current news and events. The extensive adoption of AI by Xinhua shows that it is determined to use technology to improve its news production efficiency and address a global audience.

Source: Xinhuanet Official Report

4. Caixin Media – AI-assisted Financial Journalism

The Caixin media, a major Chinese financial news organization has come up with a set of AI tools to assist in its journalism. It has developed data structuring and assisted writing systems such as "Zhi Que" and "Qun Yan" that aid journalists in processing and analyzing huge data, efficiently. In May 2025, its Caixin Caizhi text generation algorithm was officially registered by the Cyberspace Administration of China, as it complies with the rules on AI-generated content.

The AI systems that Caixin implements are especially useful in investigative journalism, and financial analysis, where the processing of large volumes of data is paramount. Through automating data extraction and preliminary analysis, the journalists at Caixin are able to work on uncovering insight and narrating the stories. This example of AI being integrated into the newsroom as part of the workflow is an example of how technology can help improve the depth and impact of financial journalism to give readers more accurate and insightful reporting.

Source: Caixin Official Announcement
