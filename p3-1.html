Misinformation Risk

How AIGC accelerates the spread of misleading content in digital journalism

The Growing Risk of AI Misinformation

With the rise of AI-generated content (AIGC), misinformation can now be created and distributed faster than ever before. Advanced generative models are capable of producing highly realistic text, images, and videos, making it increasingly difficult for audiences to distinguish between authentic and fabricated news.

Real-World Case: Deepfake of Zelensky (2022)

In 2022, during the Russia–Ukraine conflict, a deepfake video of Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky appeared online, falsely showing him urging Ukrainian troops to surrender. Although the video was quickly debunked, it demonstrated how AI-generated media can be weaponized to spread disinformation in critical situations.

The video circulated on social media platforms and raised serious concerns about the potential use of AIGC in information warfare. Even short-lived misinformation can cause confusion, panic, and damage to public trust.

Why AIGC Makes Misinformation More Dangerous

Low Production Cost

AI tools allow users to generate realistic content with minimal effort, lowering the barrier to creating fake news.

High Speed of Spread

AI-generated misinformation can be produced and shared instantly across social media, reaching large audiences before verification.

High Realism

Deepfake technology produces highly convincing visuals and audio, making fake content difficult to detect even for experts.

Impact on Journalism

The rise of AI-generated misinformation poses a significant threat to the credibility of journalism. As audiences become more skeptical of media content, trust in news organizations may decline. This creates a challenging environment where journalists must not only report the news but also actively verify and debunk false information.
