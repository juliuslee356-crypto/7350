What is AIGC in Journalism?

Exploring the core definition, technological foundations, and the paradigm shift in the modern newsroom.

Defining AIGC in Digital Media

AIGC (artificial intelligence generated content) represents a major paradigm shift in information production and distribution. In the context of news communication, it refers to the use of advanced machine learning models to automate, assist or enhance the creation of news content, covering text, audio, images and video.

Unlike the early algorithm news that relied on fixed templates (such as sports score briefings), modern AIGC is driven by deep learning networks with semantic understanding and creative synthesis capabilities. This changed the role of the "checkpoint" of the reporter, and the task shifted from drafting documents to prompt word engineering, fact-checking and ethical supervision.

Core Technologies Driving the Change

Large Language Model (LLM)

Such as GPT-4, etc., good at automated document drafting, title generation and real-time multilingual translation. They can adapt to different intonations and quickly rewrite breaking news into multiple versions suitable for newspapers or short videos.

Diffusion Model

Such as Midjourney, which is used to generate high-quality illustrations. Provide a visual background for news when you can't shoot real scenes or are restricted.

Automated Abstract

Use natural language processing technology to instantly digest hundred-page reports or financial documents to help investigators quickly extract core points from massive data.
