The Evolution of AI in News

From simple automated templates to deep generative editing and multi-modal storytelling.

Stage 1: Automated Writing and Templates (2010 - 2017)

"Robot News" was born. The Associated Press and Bloomberg began to use the NLG algorithm to convert structured data (such as financial reports and scores) into newsletters.

Example: The "earthquake robot" of the Los Angeles Times can automatically publish reports within 3 minutes after the earthquake.

Stage 2: Multimedia and AI Anchor (2018 - 2022)

AI crosses the boundaries of text. Xinhua News Agency launched the world's first AI synthetic anchor in 2018. During this period, AI began to assist video editing, automatic subtitles and dubbing. Although visually shocking, the core script still needs to be reviewed by humans.

Stage 3: Deep Generative Editing (2023 - Present)

The release of ChatGPT marks the real generative AI era. AI is now directly integrated into the news editing system. It can not only write manuscripts, but also conduct emotional analysis, predict trends, and synchronously convert the same survey report into tweets, video scripts and long articles.
